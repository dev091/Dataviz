# Pricing And Packaging Strategy

## Pricing Principles
- price on business value, not low-cost seat count
- keep entry point premium enough to support onboarding and support quality
- make automation depth and governance depth visible in packaging
- avoid custom pricing logic for every deal

All pricing below is illustrative and should be validated in real sales conversations.

## Packaging Model

### Starter
Target use:
- one business domain
- one leadership reporting workflow
- a small KPI surface

Designed for:
- first production launch
- fast proof of value

Illustrative pricing:
- `$30k-$60k` annual platform fee
- one-time onboarding fee where justified

### Growth
Target use:
- multiple departments or workspaces
- broader KPI automation
- recurring executive packs and monitoring

Illustrative pricing:
- `$60k-$120k` annual platform fee
- optional connector and automation add-ons

### Enterprise
Target use:
- multi-domain deployment
- heavier governance needs
- advanced support and deployment requirements
- private or customer-controlled environment options

Illustrative pricing:
- `$120k-$250k+` annual platform fee
- deployment, connector, governance, and support add-ons where justified

## Add-On Structure
- premium connectors
- higher automation volume
- advanced governance and trust controls
- private deployment
- premium support

## Metric For Pricing Justification
Customers should justify spend using:
- analyst hours replaced
- recurring deck and dashboard maintenance eliminated
- executive time saved
- lower reporting error rate
- faster decision cycles
- broader access without proportional analyst hiring

## Packaging Guardrails
- do not rely primarily on seat-based pricing
- do not underprice automation value
- do not give away high-support custom work in base contracts
- keep expansion paths obvious and economically clean

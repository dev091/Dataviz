# First-Year Profit Model Assumptions

## Planning Rule
This is a directional model, not a guarantee. It exists to keep product and company decisions grounded in margin discipline.

## Cost Categories
- engineering and product payroll
- customer success and onboarding payroll
- sales and GTM payroll
- cloud and infrastructure
- AI and compute usage
- support and security tooling
- contractor and legal overhead

## Margin Direction
The product should be designed to support:
- `75%+` mature gross margin
- software-first economics
- low services dependency
- bounded onboarding effort

## Practical Profit View

### If Revenue Is Too Small
Even with strong gross margin, early operating profit will be constrained by fixed team cost. That is why premium ACV and disciplined onboarding matter more than broad low-price adoption.

### If Revenue Is Premium But Services Are Heavy
Profit will still suffer if onboarding is too custom. That is why productized onboarding packs and migration helpers are not optional. They are margin features.

### If Revenue Is Premium And Onboarding Is Productized
The business can support strong economics with:
- fewer delivery hours per customer
- lower support load
- faster implementation
- better expansion potential

## Operating Guardrails
- avoid high-support bespoke deployments as the norm
- keep implementation mostly product-led
- charge for premium deployment and premium support when needed
- monitor AI and compute cost as a percent of ARR

## Headcount Efficiency Direction
The company should aim to improve toward:
- higher ARR per employee over time
- low customer success hours per live customer
- low support burden after onboarding

The roadmap should be evaluated partly on whether it increases or decreases these outcomes.

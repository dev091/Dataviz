# Unit Economics Assumptions

## Planning Rule
These are design targets. They are not validated until real customers, deployments, and renewals exist.

## Target Economics
- minimum viable ACV target: `$30k-$60k`
- preferred expansion ACV target: `$75k-$150k+`
- onboarding SLA target: first executive pack in `<= 14 days`
- trusted KPI layer target: `<= 30 days`
- implementation ceiling for standard customers: `<= 40 hours`
- support ceiling after onboarding: low-touch and margin-positive
- gross margin target: `75%+`
- services-to-software ratio target: software-led, services as bounded accelerator

## Retention And Expansion Direction
- product should support strong renewal through recurring workflow replacement
- expansion should come from more departments, more automation, more governance, and more connectors
- the ideal commercial model supports healthy net revenue retention, not just logo count

## CAC And Payback Direction
The company should aim for:
- controlled sales complexity
- onboarding effort that does not destroy payback
- a sales motion where premium ACV supports reasonable payback periods

## Headcount Efficiency Assumptions
- product and engineering must reduce implementation burden over time
- customer success should guide, not rebuild reports manually
- support should stay low because trust, onboarding, and migration are productized

## Product Implications
Any roadmap item that:
- increases implementation effort materially
- adds support burden
- weakens trust
- lowers willingness to pay
- or behaves like custom services

should be deprioritized unless it clearly improves retention or expansion enough to justify the cost.

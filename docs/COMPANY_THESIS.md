# Company Thesis

## Core Thesis
Most companies do not need another dashboard canvas. They need a trusted system that replaces recurring reporting labor, reduces KPI monitoring overhead, and delivers leadership-ready outputs without depending on constant analyst intervention.

## Why Incumbents Leave an Opening
Incumbent BI products are powerful, but they still leave customers with recurring work:
- dashboards have to be built and maintained manually
- executive packs are assembled outside the BI tool
- KPI definitions drift across teams
- alerting and narrative explanation are weak or fragmented
- adoption depends heavily on analyst bandwidth

That creates an opportunity for a product that is not primarily a visualization surface, but an automated executive reporting and KPI operating system.

## Why This Wedge Matters
Executive reporting is:
- recurring
- painful
- expensive
- visible to leadership
- easier to price around business value

That makes it a stronger starting wedge than trying to support every analytics workflow on day one.

## Moat Direction
The likely moat is not one model call or one dashboard. It is the combination of:
- governed KPI semantics
- trust and explainability
- fast first-run outputs
- migration leverage from incumbent BI assets
- productized onboarding
- repeated automation in high-value reporting cycles

## Operating Thesis
To maximize the probability of strong outcomes, the company should be run with these rules:
- optimize for premium ACV, not vanity user count
- keep onboarding productized
- keep support burden low
- keep services tightly bounded
- make every new feature justify itself economically
- focus on one painful wedge until expansion is earned

## Proof Obligations
This thesis is only strong if the company can prove:
- first executive-ready output is fast
- KPI outputs are trusted
- recurring reporting labor falls materially
- deployment is repeatable
- customers expand after initial rollout

## Risks
The main risks are:
- overbuilding toward generic BI parity
- underinvesting in trust and governance
- requiring too much manual services work
- weak migration leverage from incumbent BI
- poor packaging that underprices automation value

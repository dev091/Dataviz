# Why Customers Switch From Incumbents

## The Real Switching Story
Customers do not switch because they want one more dashboard tool. They switch when the current stack still leaves them paying for too much manual work.

## Primary Switch Triggers
- dashboards require constant upkeep
- executive packs are still built manually in slides or spreadsheets
- KPI definitions drift across teams
- alerting is weak or not trusted
- business users depend too heavily on analysts
- leadership wants explanations, not just charts

## What This Product Must Be Better At
- faster time to first executive-ready output
- lower recurring maintenance burden
- stronger trust and explainability
- more proactive KPI monitoring
- more repeatable onboarding
- better leadership usability

## What Not To Sell As The Main Advantage
- more charts
- more menus
- more knobs
- more feature sprawl
- vague AI assistant claims

## Migration Leverage Required To Win
- connector-first onboarding
- semantic model bootstrap from existing datasets
- KPI definition import helpers
- dashboard and report mapping assistants
- trust comparison against incumbent outputs
- first executive pack fast workflow

## Competitive Narrative
The product should be framed as:
- the system that automates the reporting work surrounding BI
- the trust layer for leadership metrics
- the operating system for recurring KPI intelligence

## Proof Needed In Sales
- a prospect connects data and gets meaningful outputs quickly
- outputs match trusted definitions closely enough to use
- executive summaries reduce prep time materially
- alerts and digests show change without extra analyst effort

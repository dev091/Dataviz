# ICP And Buyer Map

## Primary ICP
The first ICP is:
- mid-market and lower enterprise companies
- roughly 200 to 5000 employees
- multiple business systems
- recurring leadership reporting pain
- overloaded analytics or BI teams
- executive sponsorship potential
- willingness to pay for automation and trust
- governance and security requirements

## Best Early Customer Shape
The best early customers are companies that already have:
- a data warehouse or at least a few critical systems
- recurring weekly and monthly reporting rituals
- metric inconsistency across teams
- leadership frustration with reporting lag
- a team that wants fewer manual deck and dashboard cycles

## Buyer Map

### Economic Buyer
- CFO
- COO
- CRO
- VP Finance
- GM with heavy operating review cadence

Primary concern:
- reduce recurring labor and improve management visibility

### Technical Buyer
- Head of Data
- BI lead
- analytics engineering leader
- IT or data platform owner

Primary concern:
- data trust, security, maintainability, and deployment burden

### Champion
- RevOps lead
- FP&A lead
- analytics manager
- chief of staff
- operations leader who owns recurring reporting

Primary concern:
- eliminate manual reporting work and ship trusted outputs faster

### End Users
- executives
- finance leaders
- RevOps leaders
- operations leaders
- analytics teams

Primary concern:
- clear, trusted, low-friction answers and automated reporting

## Procurement And Security Concerns
- data security posture
- connector permissions
- auditability
- accuracy and trust of automated metrics
- deployment model
- contract structure

## Implementation Stakeholders
- analytics
- data engineering
- IT and security
- business system owners
- executive sponsor

## Expansion Stakeholders
- department heads
- FP&A
- sales leadership
- operations leadership
- customer success leadership

## Early Qualification Filters
Prioritize prospects that have:
- repeated executive reporting pain
- at least one overloaded analytics function
- multiple reporting systems
- leadership review cadence every week or month
- willingness to standardize KPI definitions
